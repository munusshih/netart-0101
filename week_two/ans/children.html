Born Taiwanese!

In your definition, people who are born in Taiwan are considered Taiwanese regardless of their ancestors. It is interesting because Taiwanese laws base citizenship in jus sanguinis (Latin for "right of blood") instead of jus soli (Latin for "right of soil/territory").The same definition however could be held against first-generation Taiwanese and other immigrants in Taiwan.

This argument meant a lot to the Taiwanese people who were born on the island during the Japanese colonial rule. Some of them thought they would gain independence after the Japanese retreated in World War II, and the Taiwanese Born can finally rule themselves. However, the land was taken over by another foregin government again, the Chinese government Republic of China (ROC), who flee from China after losing in the Chinese civil war.

Only until 1990 when President Lee Teng-huiL became the first democratically elected president in Taiwan, it was an important moment in history given that he was the first president who was born and raised in Taiwan.

What Taiwanese Are We Talking About by Munus Shih, Weikai Cheng
